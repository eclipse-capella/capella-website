Behavioural components FIP 4_1 Check each engineering/architecting decision against physical viewpoints

A behavioural component is a part of the final breakdown of the system (at the current engineering level), carying the functional and behavioural expectations described in the functional/non-functional need.

Several components may be grouped, for complexity management and separation of concerns purpose, into one father component, so as to manage complexity and hide details inside the component. This leads to a component tree structuring the components(also known as component breakdown).
